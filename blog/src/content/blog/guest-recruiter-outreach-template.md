When I reach out cold, the messages that get replies almost always do three things:

1. **Name the role and level precisely** — not "exciting opportunity at a fast-growing startup"
2. **Cite one reason you matched them** — stack, domain, or a public artifact you actually read
3. **Make the next step low-friction** — a 15-minute call window or a link to a specific posting

Candidates aren't ignoring recruiters because they're busy. They're ignoring messages that could have been sent to five hundred people.
